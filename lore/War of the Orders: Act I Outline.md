# War of the Orders: Act I Outline
## Prologue<!-- {"fold":true} -->

### Opening Atmosphere (Tension & Setting)
* The sun slides low, casting the Murhaelis Sea in bronze and blood-red bands. Sky haze blurs horizon and cloud, as if dusk itself were smoldering.
* A half-dozen Qora’thular grain-ships ride heavy in the water, their holds packed with wheat. Flanked by a pair of escorts, their decks bristle with outdated cannon, enough for deterrence but not for pitched battle.
* Sailors scrub decks, mend lines, and curse the long haul east. They expect a quiet run before nightfall. The sea is calm, winds steady.
* Some mutter of furnace-ships seen in other waters, of Kaethrin raiders who vanish as quickly as they strike. Younger sailors laugh it off, but the old hands spit over the rail and fall silent.
* **Foreshadowing:** Slowly, the signs change. The gulls that had circled all day vanish. The wind turns faint and sour. An acrid tang creeps into the air, and sailors taste ash at the back of their throats.

### First Signs
* **Early Omen (subtle):** At first it seems only imagination—the faint whiff of sulfur, the sharp sting of ash-dust in the nose. Old hands mutter, spitting over the rails. “Bad wind. Bad omen.”
* **Recognition (too late):** A veteran, face pale in the red half-light, whispers: *“The ash has caught us.”* The phrase spreads quickly, a sailor’s shorthand for the Kaethrin fleet. Fear ripples through the decks.
* **Sudden Escalation:** Lookouts squint to horizon and see it at last—thin streaks of ember-light rising from the haze, ash-plumes bleeding into dusk. The glow spreads, and with it, dread certainty: the Kaethrin are near.

### Kaethrin Approach
* **Drifting Shapes:** At first they look like hulks, shadows adrift, sails bound tight. Then the line sharpens: low black hulls riding steady, furnace-cores banked so dim they resemble dying coals. They move deceptively slow, drifting like carcasses.
* **Stirring Runes:** Only when the distance closes do the carvings etched into their obsidian plating flare awake. Ember-red glyphs crawl across their sides, vent-cages glowing faintly like volcanic seams. To the convoy, it is as though eruptions have risen from the sea itself.
* **Dread Realized:** Lanterns are lit too late, guns wheeled about in panic. The Kaethrin are closing for the kill.

### The Attack
* **Ember-Skimmers:** Fast, ember-driven craft sweep in on the flanks. Furnace-lances roar, jets of incandescent flame punching through escort hulls, melting timbers and blistering iron. Convoy escorts reel under the onslaught.
* **Rune-Chains:** Steam-pneumatic launchers thump, chains arc across the dusk and snap onto grain-ships’ rails and masts. Crews scramble to cut them loose, but the links bite deep, locking prey to predator.
* **Molten Bombards:** From the rear, magma-barges lob slag-shells high, bursting on decks in cascades of molten stone and fire. Grain sacks ignite; rails splinter under the weight of slag.
* **Smoke-Casters:** Ash-plumes roll low across the water, choking sailors, blinding gunners. Order falters in the convoy as captains lose sight of one another.
* **Boarding Ships:** Black-prowed assault launches surge from the shadows, furnace-jets blasting short bursts that push them into ramming speed. Their reinforced bows slam into grain-ships already chained. Gang-planks crash down, and Kaethrin marines vault aboard in a storm of sparks and ember-light, chanting as they strike.

### Destruction
* **Shattered Escort:** One convoy escort, hull torn open by furnace lances, rolls under as its magazine catches. The blast tears a plume of fire into the sky, silhouetting the chaos in stark orange. Survivors leap into the sea, only to choke on ash-thick water.
* **Grain-Hold Inferno (Unintended):** A slag-shell from a magma-barge strikes a grain ship by mistake, bursting into the deck bound in rune-chains. The hold ignites, wheat and rye going up in choking, oily flame.
* **The Taken Ship:** Another grain-ship, successfully captured, is boarded and seized. Marines drag crew screaming into pyres lit on their own deck—bodies immolated as offerings. Cargo is seized under Ashpriests’ watch.
* **Smoke and Panic:** Convoy order collapses entirely. Crews stumble blind in choking haze, captains screaming conflicting orders. Some ships collide in confusion, others cut anchors to flee. None can see the horizon, only the glow of their ships burning around them.
* **Ash on Water:** Flames leap from ship to ship, sails collapsing in showers of sparks. The sea itself seems aflame: oil and splinters burn on the surface, turning the waves into black-and-red mirrors. Dead float among glowing embers, water seething around their still forms.
* **Departure of the Ash-Fleet:** As the convoy collapses, Kaethrin warships pivot away in disciplined rhythm. One grain-ship smolders as a ruin. Another grain-ship, chained and overrun, is seized as prize. A Kaethrin crew takes the helm as it turns in formation with the Ashen-Fleet. Behind them, smoke-pillars rise skyward, red reflection painting the dusk. They do not linger to finish every enemy.

## Chapter 1<!-- {"fold":true} -->
The *Vel Aurvar*

[[Arrival in the Murhaelis]]

*From Sevran Kalethor’s perspective*

### Scene I — The Approach (1,200–1,500 words)
* **Sea & Sky:** Southern Murhaelis calm, its surface rolling in long indigo swells. Shafts of late light broke through the haze. Warm winds on the southeastern trade winds. The archipelago looms northward, jagged and half-veiled. One **charred plank** drifts past Sevran’s vessel — unspoken reminder of the Qora’thular convoy’s fate.
* **Duvvrah Vessel:** Medium-sized, pale desert oak reinforced with ash-stone, sails dyed in muted mineral hues. No pomp, no ornament. Decks hushed except for wind and rigging.
* **Delegation Arrangement:** Sevran sits cross-legged under a canvas awning with aides, a Silent Prefect, and guards. Murmurs ripple when the plank passes; Sevran silences them.
* **Observation:** He reflects that diplomacy begins before a word is spoken: *even silence is posture, even arrival a clause.*

### Scene II — First Sight of the Vel Aurvar (1,200–1,500 words)
* **Flagship Emerges:** From haze rises the *Vel Aurvar*, immense, iron-hulled, plated in blackmirror, gleaming bronze-green. Its scale dwarfs Sevran’s ship; its stillness makes the sea look small.
* **Details of Awe:** Beacon-Hall stern like a cathedral, six masts with black sails furled high, gilded prow etched with contract-glyphs. Paddle wheels churn steady, smoke vented through gilded cowls glowing red.
* **Crew Reactions:** Duvvrah sailors fall silent, unsettled by the scale. A scribe whispers that “it moves less like a ship than a wall.” Sevran notes the Renn’s purpose: intimidation masked as hospitality.
* **Internal Reflection:** He recognizes the trap: to even step aboard is to accept the ledger — *hospitality itself as debt.*

### Scene III — Boarding Ritual (800–1,000 words)
* **Renn Launches:** Silent rowboats manned by Renn sailors in oil-dyed coats ferry delegations. Weapons forbidden.
* **Guest Ledger:** Before boarding, Sevran and his envoys must inscribe their names in **laurelfin-dust ink**, glowing faintly as it bonds to the page. Even entry is made contract.
* **Boarding the Flagship:** Gangways rise; Renn guards watch with ritual stillness. The ship’s uncanny steadiness unsettles Sevran further. *“Not even the sea may move without their consent,”* he thinks.
* **First Glimpse:** As they’re led to quarters, Sevran glimpses the **Grand Ledger Hall** — blackmirror floors, volcanic glass table gleaming. He feels a knot of dread: every word tomorrow will echo here.

### Scene IV — First Encounters (1,200–1,500 words)<!-- {"fold":true} -->
* **The Qora’thular Delegation:**
* Already installed. Their rooms are plain but placed beside the Ylvath’mul.
* Envoys carry the raw weight of loss: their cities burning, their grain convoys gone.
* Sevran exchanges formal courtesies. He sees their desperation, but also their fear that no one will heed them without Order backing.
* **The Ylvath’mul Delegation:**
* Arrive in fungal robes of green, purple, ochre. The air around them smells of damp loam and fermentation.
* They speak with calm gravity. A Veridic priest murmurs: *“Ash feeds soil, soil blooms anew.”* Sevran recognizes both kinship and distance.
* **The Velut’mora Delegation:**
* Smallest, quietest presence. Cloaks of mist-woven fabric, fungal shell growths. They speak rarely, but when they do, their words feel weighted with “Deep Memory.”
* Their presence signals that even minor archipelago peoples fear Kaethril expansion.
* **Dynamic:** Sevran observes alliances already forming. Qora’thular lean visibly on Ylvath’mul support. Velut’mora stand nearer to them, not him. Sevran senses the Duvvrah will not dominate the “ally bloc.”

### Scene V — Among His Own (800–1,000 words)<!-- {"fold":true} -->
* **Private Chambers:** Midship suites — small, blackmirror-paneled, maritime murals that double as subtle claims of Renn reach. Sevran notes the manipulation: his suite placed opposite Qora’thular, beside Ylvath’mul.
* **Internal Discussion:**
* The Silent Prefect urges detachment: *“Do not cling to loss or alliance. Speak only what release allows.”*
* A younger aide argues they must bind themselves closer to Qora’thular and Ylvath’mul, lest Kaethril and Renn dominate.
* Guards remain silent, but Sevran notices their hands tight on their *sevrel* hilts — even they feel the tension.
* **Sevran’s Mediation:** He tempers them: alliances are useful but dangerous if too rigid. “What the Renn offer is stage, not sanctuary.”

### Scene VI — Sevran’s Reflections (1,000–1,200 words)
* **Evening Solitude:** Alone in his quarters, Sevran writes notes on ash-pigment scrolls (to be burned after). His reflections are sharper:
* The Renn’s ship as contract — *every step, every glance part of their ledger.*
* The Qora’thular as tragic — but weak without Order weight.
* The Ylvath’mul as unsettling allies — their calm rotted through with spores.
* The Velut’mora as spectral — reminding him that even the smallest peoples are drawn into the maw of war.
* **Closing Image:** He stares from the porthole at the sea, slate-dark under the Beacon-Hall’s glow. In the distance, another Renn escort cuts silently through the water. He wonders how much choice remains to any of them — or if all are already inside the Renn ledger.

### Chapter Arc
* **Purpose:** This chapter establishes Sevran’s voice, Duvvrah philosophy of release vs. entanglement, the atmosphere of Renn stagecraft, and the early alignments of delegations.
* **Wordcount Potential:** 6,000–7,500 words comfortably, by leaning into **observation, atmosphere, layered description, and quiet conversation.**
* **Foreshadowing:** The absences of Kaethril and Lavan — explicitly noted — build tension for Chapter 2.

## Chapter 2<!-- {"fold":true} -->

### Scene I — The Approach (En route to Vel Aurvar) 700<!-- {"fold":true} -->

*Parallel to Sevran at Sea, but cerebral where his was physical.*
* **Setting:** The sleek, dark Lavan vessel cuts across the Murhaelis. Their ship is a contrast to Sevran’s weathered vessel — no wasted ornament, every line purposeful. Moralen notes this as emblematic of their philosophy.
* **Conversation with Valserin Drovath (lead diplomat):**
* They rehearse their “entrance lines” with cold precision, like musicians testing harmony.
* Valserin insists: they must project inevitability, not invitation — they arrive because order demands it.
* Stakes: If they can force the Renn to police Resiluth, they control both the narrative *and* the trade dynamic.
* **Moralen’s internal lens:**
* She admires Valserin’s poise but has her own more fervent convictions: she actually believes harmony is a *sacred principle*, not just a tool.
* She frames the sea around them — churning currents, chaotic tide lines — as a metaphor for unchecked transformation. It disgusts her, compared to the purity of measured form.
* She admits to herself that she *wants* the others to be destabilized. Shock isn’t just a tactic — it is the truest test of control.
* **Expansion device:** Use vivid sensory description of the sea, the vessel, and Moralen’s restrained anticipation to **match Sevran’s action-heavy opener with intellectual tension**.

### Scene II — The Arrival 600<!-- {"fold":true} -->

*Parallel to Sevran’s docking, but charged with social reaction.*
* **Dockside reaction:** Guards, dockhands, aides scramble to hide their shock. Small failures: dropped ropes, delayed salutes, glances too quickly averted. The Lavan catalog every detail.
* **The disembarkation:**
* Delegates descend in *perfect unison*, robes falling in mirrored folds. Compared to the rough edges of the dockworkers, they look like living diagrams.
* Their presence is *designed to humiliate* without words — a geometry of control against human mess.
* **Speculation spreads:** whispers ripple. Some aides mutter the Renn must have orchestrated this.
* **Moralen’s perception:**
* She studies the Renn closely. Sevran’s subtle look to Lanis shows *they too were caught off guard* — a quiet triumph for the Lavan.
* She savors the moment: the lie of Renn control is fractured before the delegations even begin.
* **Expansion device:**
* Add layers of observation — how each flicker of shock betrays alliances and expectations.
* Linger on Moralen’s enjoyment of the gap between mask and truth.

### Scene III — The Reception Hall 800

*Parallel to Sevran’s entry, but here the tension spikes higher.*
* **The stride-in:** The Lavan enter unannounced, their footsteps echoing with unnatural precision. The hall *stillness* is almost theatrical.
* **Reactions from each Order (Moralen filters them like “instruments out of tune”):**
* **Renn:** outward serenity, but their eyes betray calculation under pressure.
* **Qora’thular:** revulsion hidden in intellectual detachment. Their disdain is palpable, but weaponized into silence.
* **Duvvrah:** restraint slipping — a flicker of discomfort at their exclusion.
* **Ylvath’mul:** smiling faintly, watching the others squirm.
* **Moralen’s frame:** She imagines herself in a conductor’s seat — dissonant instruments awaiting her hand.
* **Expansion device:**
* Use pauses, stillness, and Moralen’s internal commentary to stretch this scene into a **slow-burning theatrical reveal**, like the silence before an orchestra begins.

### Scene IV — Confrontation with the Renn 700

*The pivot moment, mirroring Sevran’s exchanges but sharper and faster.*
* **Renn attempt control:** They step forward, poised, demanding clarity: “This gathering was convened without you.”
* **Valserin’s line:** pitched for *all ears, not just Renn* — “We are here because Resiluth makes every sword sharper, every fire brighter, every wound deeper. Without us, these talks will circle chaos.”
* **Room’s reaction:**
* Some Orders murmur agreement, or at least acknowledgment.
* Qora’thular seize the chance to glare at the Renn.
* Brakka grumble about smuggling openly.
* **The Renn falter:** insist smuggling is not their burden. Their denial looks weak.
* **Moralen’s perspective:**
* Notes with delight how quickly the initiative shifted.
* Watches Sevran and Lanis trade minute glances, trying to catch balance.
* **Expansion device:**
* Draw out the exchange with **layered dialogue**: Valserin’s lines like chords, Renn’s replies like broken rhythm.
* Let Moralen’s inner commentary “score” the confrontation as if it’s music being badly played.

### Scene V — Moralen’s Reflection 300

*Mirrors Sevran’s last beat but ideological instead of weary.*
* **Lingering silence:** The confrontation ends but tension hums like a struck string.
* **Moralen’s reflection:**
* The initiative belongs to the Lavan.
* She acknowledges the Renn will recover, the Qora’thular will spit venom, but for now, the “axis of order” has been established.
* For her personally: this is the stage she wants — conflict as symphony, where she can prove herself indispensable.
* **Closing image:** She imagines the negotiations as a performance, each Order an instrument. And she intends to hold the conductor’s baton.

## Chapter III: The Choir’s Bargain<!-- {"fold":true} -->

### Scene 1 – Vey Maraveth in Revolt
* **Backdrop**: The Lavan port city of Vey Maraveth is in open chaos. The Choir has risen, police and militia are fighting in the streets, and smoke rises from a sabotaged Resiluth forge. Bells, gunfire, and chanting fill the air.
* **The Deal**:
* Rescar meets the Choir in a warehouse by the docks. Half the space is stacked with crates of Resiluth nodes, half filled with wounded zealots being treated.
* The Choir insists on ritualizing the handoff, blessing every crate. Rescar pushes for speed, cracking jokes to keep the zealots compliant.
* Kael inspects crates and notes sloppy handling; vibrations could destabilize them. He quietly asks for arc lamp parts (cabling, commutator coil, carbons) as part of payment.
* The Choir is puzzled; electricity means little to them. Rescar rolls his eyes, muttering about Kael “buying street lamps while we’re buying fortunes.”
* Syl wanders and overhears zealots saying “the angels sang in the north quarter,” meaning the rebellion is striking at the heart of Lavan Resiluth production.

### Scene 2 – Loading the Fin
* Choir porters carry crates across the dock as sporadic gunfire and smoke drift close.
* Kael oversees loading: crates hidden behind false bulkheads, covered with lead-lined cloth, and masked by barrels of fish and bolts of cloth. He trims ballast hastily.
* Rescar positions Syl visibly on deck rail to sell the “merchant with daughter” disguise.
* Tension in the air: loading is rushed, sloppy, and the city is burning around them.

### Scene 3 – Departure Amidst Chaos
* The dockside is guarded only by scattered, exhausted police detachments. Their priority is holding the docks until the Third Harmonic Regiment arrives, not paperwork.
* Two officers confront Rescar as he casts off, saying no ship leaves until reinforcements arrive.
* Rescar pleads urgency: spoiled fish means starving mouths. He keeps Syl in sight.
* Before the argument can escalate, an explosion rocks a nearby warehouse — Choir saboteurs setting another fire.
* In the distraction, Rescar slips the *Fin*’s lines and pushes off under sail and low steam. Kael curses the imbalance in the hold but gets them moving.

### Scene 4 – The Regiment at Sea
* As the *Fin* clears the port, the Third Harmonic Regiment arrives by sea: multiple blackmirror-hulled corvettes and frigates.
* Most peel toward the harbor to land troops and suppress the rebellion. One corvette breaks off to intercept the fleeing *Fin*.
* Kael warns they can’t outrun them head-on. Rescar orders a wide northwest course.
* The corvette fires a warning shot, signaling them to heave to. Rescar refuses, ordering Kael to push the engine.
* Kael engages Runner mode. The *Fin* hums unnaturally as speed builds. The corvette struggles to close the gap, trying to herd them back east.
* Kael swings ballast and threads the *Fin* through shallow reef lines. The heavier corvette can’t follow.
* The *Fin* escapes west into open Murhaelis waters. The corvette fires again, shots falling short.

### Scene 5 – At Sea, Planning the Next Stop
* The ship eases down from Runner mode. Crew collapses into hammocks, shaken but alive.
* Rescar is triumphant, grinning, flipping his carved token. “Chaos breeds profit.”
* Kael fumes, noting overheated bearings, burned insulation, and sloppy ballast trim. He warns the ship won’t survive many more “sprints.”
* Rescar brushes it off. Repairs can wait; profit comes first.
* Conversation shifts to their **next destination**: a Qora’thular port further south.
* Rescar: desperate Qora’thular colonies will pay well for illicit Resiluth.
* Kael: risk of seizure if they discover Choir-cut nodes.
* Rescar: desperation makes them honest — for a price.
* Syl, half-awake, asks why they don’t just sell at home. Rescar: “Home pays in coppers, the south pays in gold.” Kael mutters: “And sometimes in blood.”
* Chapter closes on the *Fin* rocking in calm waters under moonlight, leaving smoke of Vey Maraveth far behind, heading into the next gamble.

## Chapter V: The Qora’thular’s Desperation

*Rescar → Qora’thular*
**Theme**: Exploitation disguised as lifeline.
* **Setting**: A Renn-controlled port quarter where the Qora’thular delegation languishes—docks crowded with refugee barges, the smell of smoke and famine.
* **Dynamic**:
* The Qora’thular quartermaster bargains for grain but Rescar steers them instead into buying *Resiluth* at “discount,” assuring them: *“Move it as barter, not weaponry. Grain flows faster than pity.”*
* He frames it as a solution to their crisis—“tools for trade,” while quietly pocketing their desperation.
* **Rescar’s play**: He sells them *just enough* to think they’ve secured advantage, while deliberately keeping most of the shipment. To them, he’s a savior. To himself, they’re cover. If caught by Renn inspectors, he can say the crates were meant for their allies.
* **Closing beat**: As he leaves, a Qora’thular officer mutters, *“He mocks us.”* Another silences him: *“He feeds us.”* Rescar, hearing both, smirks: *“Exactly.”*
* **Setting**: A Qora’thular port further south — practical, storm-battered, not wealthy. Their infrastructure is fraying, refugees flowing in from Choir uprisings.
* **Plot**:
* Rescar offers Resiluth to local officials. They need it for storm-walls and stabilizers, but can’t admit to buying Choir-tainted nodes.
* Kael realizes they’re not prepared to handle untuned Resiluth safely — and disaster could follow.
* Rescar presses ahead anyway, insisting their desperation makes them trustworthy.
* **Conflict**:
* The Qora’thular faction haggles fiercely, some considering seizing the cargo instead of paying.
* The *Fin*’s disguise and Syl’s presence again become crucial to the negotiation.
* Deal succeeds (for now), but Rescar’s overconfidence grows.
* **Foreshadowing Needed in Ch. III**:
* Rescar’s line about “Qora’thular desperation makes them honest — for a price.”
* Kael muttering “sometimes in blood” to hint at the cost of trusting them.

## Chapter VI: The Kaethrin’s Price

*Rescar → Kaethrin*
**Theme**: From opportunism to danger.
* **Setting**: A Kaethrin war-cove—ships moored like wolves at rest, artificers working openly on crude Resiluth devices.
* **Dynamic**:
* Rescar sells the remaining cache to a Kaethrin Warlord. Negotiations are tense: accusations of short-weighting, threats of violence.
* Rescar smooths it over with wit, claiming Renn patrols forced him to “lighten the load,” but that he’s still brought them the best cut.
* **Demonstration**: A Resiluth cannon fires without smoke, collapsing a cliff. The Kaethrin roar approval; Rescar laughs outwardly but feels unease. He thinks: *“I sell them stones. They think I sell them thrones.”*
* **Closing beat**: The Warlord pays more than Rescar asked, saying: *“Your coin is nothing. But your cargo makes us gods.”* Rescar leaves richer than ever, flipping a carved token through his fingers. In his ledger he writes: *“Qora’thular fed. Kaethrin armed. Renn still blind. Beautiful.”*
* **Setting**: A hidden cove or isolated trading post, far from Order oversight.
* **Plot**:
* Rescar secretly arranges to sell another portion of the same shipment to the Kaethrin — enemies of the Orders.
* Kael is furious — the Kaethrin won’t just use Resiluth for tools, they’ll weaponize it.
* Syl notices something chilling among the Kaethrin smugglers (perhaps Resiluth-driven firearms that look unstable, or mutilated captives).
* **Conflict**:
* The Kaethrin bring more men than agreed, intending to intimidate Rescar into a lower price.
* Rescar defuses with charm and calculated bravado, but Kael nearly blows the cover by speaking truth about how dangerous the cargo is.
* They leave alive, but Syl’s trust in Rescar begins to waver.
* **Foreshadowing Needed in Ch. III–IV**:
* Rescar dropping hints that he always has “another buyer” in mind.
* Syl overhearing Choir zealots saying their enemies (the Kaethrin) are already profiting from smuggled shipments.

## Chapter VII – Closing the Net
* **Setting**: Open waters south of the Murhaelis, where multiple Orders’ patrols overlap.
* **Plot**:
* News of smuggling routes has spread. Both Renn and Lavan ships are on heightened watch.
* A rival smuggler or Renn authority leaks word of the *Fin’s* movements.
* The *Fin* is pursued, and Kael must use Breaker mode in earnest — risking the ship’s structural collapse.
* **Conflict**:
* Hull rivets loosen, seams begin leaking, and Syl is terrified as the *Fin* sings like an instrument about to split.
* Rescar treats it as another gamble: “Every fortune is won on one throw.”
* They escape, but Kael writes in his log that the ship cannot take another run like this.
* **Foreshadowing Needed Earlier**:
* Kael warning repeatedly in Ch. III–IV about overstressing the bearings and cabling.
* The inspection officer in Ch. IV noticing how “dead calm” the ship feels.
