# War of the Orders: Character Overviews

### The Opportunist
* **Role:** Merchant, minor noble, or middle-rank officer.
* **Theme:** Treats war and collapse as a ladder; thrives in chaos, resented by all.
* **Arc:** rises astonishingly high but risks catastrophic downfall.
* **Order:** Corvas Renn
* **Introduced:** Book 1

### The Loyalist
* **Role:** Devoted soldier, monk, or official.
* **Theme:** Absolute faith in their Order’s philosophy, no matter how it falters.
* **Arc:** remains devoted to the order no matter how many suffer. Does near the end of book 3
* **Order:** Lavat Nin
* **Introduced:** Book 1

### The Caretaker
* **Role:** Healer, teacher, parent, or steward of the weak.
* **Theme:** Embodies the quiet labor of holding communities together when leaders are obsessed with glory.
* **Arc:** a constant moral compass but dues tragically during an invasion in the end of book 2
* **Order:** Kaethril Ghorai
* **Introduced:** Book 1

### The Diplomat
* **Role:** Negotiator among politicians 
* **Theme:** Desperate search for deescalation and peace
* **Arc:** Starts willing to make appeasements in hopes for deescalation, becomes certain that the enemy must be stoped at all costs. Dies in Book 3 after a Chorus detonation
* **Order:** Duvvrah Thos
* **Introduced:** Book 1

### The Soldier
* **Role:** Professional fighter, officer, or lifelong conscript.
* **Theme:** Duty as the only constant; survival and service are synonymous.
* **Arc:** starts out believing in thier order’s mission but is driven to madness (ptsd)
* **Order:** Ylvath’mul
* **Introduced:** Book 2

### The General
* **Role:** Senior commander, war-leader, or strategist elevated during the Great War.
* **Theme:** Burden of command — every order means lives spent. Believes victory is possible, but wrestles with what kind of world such victory creates.
* **Arc:** starts as a well respected and proud officer, ends haunted by the soldiers that died on his behalf
* **Order:** Tsari Lorn
* **Introduced:** Book 2

### The Child of War
* **Role:** A youth born at the start of the trilogy, shaped entirely by collapse.
* **Theme:** Embodies the cost of war across generations.
* **Arc:** goes through stages of support, disillusionment, rebellion, acceptance, and then dies mid book 3
* **Order:** Brakka-Gnoth
* **Introduced:** Book 2
