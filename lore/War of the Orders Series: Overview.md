# War of the Orders Series: Overview

## Book I — Prelude to the Great War of Orders<!-- {"fold":true} -->

### World Events
**Resiluth Trade**
* Major: Lavat Nin attempts to monopolize the resiluth trade, presenting it as both sacred harmony and rightful dominion.
* Major: Corvas Renn merchants, with backing from underground western markets, undercut Lavat authority in the east.
* Minor: The resulting smuggling networks inflame tensions across Orders.
* Minor: Brakkal food surpluses are necessary for sustaining quality of life in Veylathun. The Lavan negotiate a trade  deal to trade resiluth for food without Corvas involvement as typical middlemen
**Southwestern War**
* Major: Kaethril Ghorai expand outward, seizing colonies and taking land from the Qora’thular in an ongoing war
* Major: Ylvath citizens are killed in Qora’thular territory 
* Minor: Renn and Duvvrah vessels are sized or destroyed 
* Minor: Tsari citizens see the Kaethril as unjust actors, and push for aid or military involvement.

**Civil Unrest**
* Major: Ylvath citizens demand involvement as the southern war has killed their own and threatens the conquest of their closest ally
* Minor: Remnents of the Choir of Inarith try to revive revolution in Lavat
* Minor: Kaethril subjects in eastern colonies rebel

### Characters
**Loyalist (Lavat Nin):**
* Introduced as high ranking government official with influence on military technology.
* Frames unrest as proof the Hierarchy must tighten and Lavat must expand dominion
* Insists on involvement in the southwestern war to restore order under Lavan rule
**Diplomat (Duvvrah Thos):**
* facilitates uneasy treaties and trade pacts with the Kaethril, encouraging appeasements to reduce tensions.
* Grows increasingly aware that compromise is failing as the Kaethril are gaining dominance and momentum 
**Caretaker (Kaethril Ghorai):**
* Tends the wounded Kaethrin soldiers, seeing the losses firsthand
* Provides the human face of survival in an age of pride.
* Serves as early moral compass, criticizing Kaethril expansion as destabilizing and dangerous to children who must inherit its legacy.
**Opportunist (Corvas Renn):**
* Born a minor merchant, ascends through charm and ruthlessness.
* Exploits famine relief, smuggling corridors, and border instability to climb socially and politically.
* Resented by rivals for low class background, but is too resourceful to dislodge.

## Book II — The Great War (10–25 years of conflict)<!-- {"fold":true} -->

### World Events
* **Outbreak:** Failed negotiations spark open war. First campaigns are brutal but indecisive.
* **Escalation:** Insect surges in Brakka forests, volcanic unrest in Kaethril lands, famine in Tsari plains. Each Order interprets disaster as divine sanction.
* **Turning Point:** A major invasion sweeps across multiple territories, reshaping alliances and entrenching the Orders into total war.

### Characters
* **General (Tsari Lorn):**
* Introduced as proud commander.
* Gains early victories, earns loyalty of soldiers.
* Burden deepens as campaigns drag on, casualties mount.
* **Soldier (Ylvath’mul):**
* Introduced as stalwart fighter.
* Experiences horror of trench, plague, and endless attrition.
* Begins psychological descent — haunted by visions of rot and decay.
* **Child of War (Brakka-Gnoth):**
* Born in Book I, emerges as adolescent POV.
* Raised on vigilance and survival drills.
* Starts hopeful, then grows disillusioned as war devours youth and community.
* **Caretaker (Kaethril Ghorai):**
* Holds to compassion during first years of war.
* Provides sanctuary for orphans and displaced.
* Dies tragically in invasion climax — her loss a symbol of Kaethril overreach and the death of moral balance.
* **Opportunist (Corvas Renn):**
* Reaches peak influence, trading in famine, armaments, and intelligence.
* Builds wealth on suffering, secures power even as war rages.
* **Diplomat (Duvvrah Thos):**
* Sees appeasement fail spectacularly.
* Witnesses betrayal, atrocity, and collapse of talks.
* Hardens into belief that war must now be pursued to the end.
* **Loyalist (Lavat Nin):**
* Doubles faith in Hierarchy as war worsens.
* Willing to enforce brutal measures, convinced obedience is salvation.

**Themes:** escalation into total war, disillusionment of idealists, profiteering at its peak, and first major deaths. The loss of the Caretaker at Book II’s climax signals the end of innocence.

## Book III — Reckoning (25–40 years, end and aftermath)<!-- {"fold":true} -->

### World Events
* **Final Phase:** War consumes all. Alliances collapse into last desperate pushes.
* **Breaking Point:** Orders fracture internally — betrayals, mutinies, schisms.
* **Ceasefire / Collapse:** The war ends not in triumph but exhaustion. Lands left scarred; Orders shattered but still standing.

### Characters
* **Opportunist (Corvas Renn):**
* Climactic downfall — betrayed, overreaches, or undone by schemes.
* His fall mirrors collapse of self-serving ambition.
* **Loyalist (Lavat Nin):**
* Dies unbroken in faith.
* His final act is obedience to Order, even as it means destruction.
* Symbol of a philosophy that demanded all and gave nothing back.
* **Diplomat (Duvvrah Thos):**
* Fully hardened into a hawk.
* Lives on, but changed into a mirror of those they once opposed.
* Represents tragedy of principle consumed by war.
* **Soldier (Ylvath’mul):**
* Lost to madness/PTSD.
* Ends in broken self-destruction or vanishes into wilderness.
* Embodiment of war’s cost on the body.
* **General (Tsari Lorn):**
* Survives but hollowed, haunted by countless deaths under his command.
* Lives on as the ghost of war’s conscience.
* **Child of War (Brakka-Gnoth):**
* Dies mid-Book III in culmination of arc.
* Their death is the ultimate proof of generational sacrifice — a world consumed before the future could inherit it.

**Themes:** reckoning with generational ruin. Collapse is complete: ambition undone, faith made obsolete, compassion dead, peace warped, duty broken, command hollowed, and the future sacrificed.
